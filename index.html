    About Us

    We are a Canadian community of Egyptians and Egypt enthusiasts in BC. The Egyptian Canadian Cultural Society of BC (ECCS-BC) is a dynamic non-profit organization dedicated to gathering and serving all BC residents of Egyptian heritage, as well as individuals and groups with a genuine interest in Egyptian culture, traditions, history, and future. Our mission is to connect, develop, support, and promote the interests of all our members and friends.

    The first General Assembly meeting was held on October 6, 1979, leading to the formation of the "Egyptian Canadian Society," which was registered in Victoria under number 15346. The founding board members included Ahmed Farid Fadl, Mahmoud Sobhy Khalil, Abdel Salam El-Gemeei, Ragheb Ashour, Mahasen Hassan, and Abdallah Sedky.

    On March 18, 1980, the name was changed to the "Nile-Fraser Egyptian Canadian Society of BC," under registration number S-15346. Finally, on March 21, 2011, we adopted our current name, while retaining "Nile-Fraser" as the name of our newsletter.

    Our Egyptian Arabic School began in 1983, with the first class held on September 24, 1983, at The Pakistan Center in Vancouver, serving 11 students. The pioneering committee members were Ahmed El Neweihi, Mahmoud Sobhi Khalil, Zakaria El Ramly, Nagat El Essily, and Abdel Magid Mousa. Today, the Egyptian Arabic School operates in Burnaby, BC, offering full services to all our members and friends.

    We are delighted by your interest in our community and hope you enjoy your visit to our website.

    OUR VISION

    To be the hub organization that gathers and serves all BC resident Egyptians and other individuals and groups with a genuine interest in the Egyptian mosaic of culture, traditions, history, and future.

    OUR VALUES

    We respect diverse ideologies, religions, traditions and backgrounds.
    We honor voluntarism as our source of power, We believe in community service and service beyond self.
    We ensure transparency and integrity in all what we do.
