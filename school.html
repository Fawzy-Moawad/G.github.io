Your Egyptian Arabic School (EAS) is here…!
EAS is one of the Community Service Projects sponsored by the Egyptian Canadian Cultural Society of BC (ECCS-BC), and is Funded by donations.
Classes are organised by age-groups and levels, from the age of 4 up to 16. (adult classes will start next school year)
EAS’ classes and activities are being held every Saturday 10:30 to 2:30 PM, at ” 800 E Broadway, Vancouver, BC V5T 1Y1.
Our objective is to renew our traditional model of the previous Egyptian Arabic School, that was suspended for a very long time, by developing and conducting a multi-site weekend Schooling System that serves ECCS-BC Members and Friends in various areas in BC, starting with Burnaby, as a central location, and by making it the weekly Fun Day for every kid in school !
ECCS-BC’s vision for EAS is to develop and utilize an innovative educational system to teach the Arabic language by providing an Activity-based, Personalized, and Easily-accessible learning experience to all members and friends of ECCS-BC.
We aim to serve you with DISTINCTION through a unique top-level service with the following features:
Professionally selected Activity-based curriculum, for an enjoyable and lasting learning experience, Limited number of students per teacher/instructor, Limited number of students per classroom, Innovative progressive planning, for present easy access, and future satellite and remote service.

2023-2024 School Year:

Program for 2023 shall be in 2 terms.
First Starts on 9th of September 2023 and ends in January 2024.
Second term starts in February 2024
The school activities are being funded by member donations, and run by volunteering teachers and administrators.
Suggested donations to cover the budgeted running costs for this term are as follows: First child $ 275, siblings $ 250, plus first time registration $ 25, and books $ 50.
Registration is a one-time payment per child, and books may be used for more than one term.

EAS History in brief:

The Society started its Egyptian Arabic School in 1983, with the first class held on September 24, 1983, with 11 students, and was held in The Pakistan Center in Vancouver.
First School Committee members then were the PIONEERS: Ahmed El Neweihi, Mahmoud Sobhi Khalil, Zakaria El Ramly, Nagat El Essily and Abdel Magid Mousa.
